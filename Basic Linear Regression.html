📖 Key Terms & Definitions¶

  • Variation (Spread): This refers to the differences or "wiggle" in the Actual Temperature (y). It describes how much the temperatures vary from a flat, unchanging line.
    • The Mystery: The actual temperature (y) has a lot of "mystery wiggle." We don't know why it goes up and down.
    • The Explanation: We check the Forecast (X) to see if its wiggle matches the truth. Since they match so well, we say the Forecast (X) "explains" the mystery.

📝 Cast of Characters¶

  1. X (Forecasted Temp): This is our Predictor (the input). "The forecasted temperature (X) is used to explain the movement of the actual temperature."
  2. y (Actual Temp): This is our Observed Truth (the target). "We are studying the variation in the actual observed temperature (y)."
  3. y_pred (Model Guess): This is our Predicted Value (the output). "The predicted temperatures (y_pred) represent the red line—the best guess the model can make."
  4. R-squared (0.9757): This is our Score. "97.57% of the variation in the actual temperature (y) is explained by the forecasted temp (X)."
  5. MSE / RMSE: This is our Error. "The typical error (typical distance) between the observed (y) and predicted (y_pred) temperatures is 1.29 °F."
